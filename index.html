🏁 OCR Lev — Race Day
Jindřichův Hradec · Military Base · Anetka's Name Day
Race
Warm-up (20 min before start)
5 min easy jog → dynamic mobility (leg swings, hip openers, arm circles) → 3 × 100m build-up strides → shake out. HR elevated, body warm, nothing taxed.
Race Execution
First 1km: Adrenaline will push you to 170+ immediately. Let it pass, settle to 155–165 bpm. Running at 175 in the first km kills your last 3km.

Between obstacles: Cruise at 155–165 bpm. You've trained here for 10 days. It's familiar now.

Into obstacles: Don't brake early. Arrive fast, breathe once, execute.

Out of obstacles: Accelerate immediately. Back to 155+ bpm within 15 seconds. This is your trained edge — most people jog it off. You don't.

Last 2km: If HR is under 170, you've been conservative — open it up now.
Final km
Get to 175+ bpm and hold it to the line. You trained to have something here. Use all of it.
Target vs Last Year
Last year: 1:14:08 total · 6:28/km moving · never sprinted · finished with juice.
Today: sub-5:30/km moving pace · sub-63 min total · nothing left at the finish.

Post-Run Protocol — Every Session
Within 5 min of finishing: Walk — don't stop dead. HR down over 5 min of movement.

Stretch minimum 10 min: Hip flexors 90 sec each · Hamstrings 60 sec each · Calf/Achilles 60 sec each · Thoracic rotation 10 reps each side.

Cold shower: 2–3 min cold after hard sessions. Reduces inflammation, speeds tissue recovery.
Legs Management
Foam roll daily: Calves, quads, IT band. 5 min before bed on training days.

Legs-up-the-wall: 10 min after long runs (May 4, 7, 11, 13). Drains fluid, accelerates recovery.

Warning signs: Shin splints, knee tracking issues, sharp calf tightness — back off immediately. 90% healthy beats 100% injured.
Nervous System
Sprint work and plyometrics (May 5, 9) are CNS-intensive. You'll feel muscularly fine but flat the next day — normal.

HRV on Garmin: If it drops 10+ points below your baseline, treat that day as recovery regardless of the plan.
Taper Week — May 14–15
Taper paranoia is real. Heavy, slow, stiff legs = glycogen loading. This is good. Do not add sessions to feel better — worst thing you can do.

Stay off your feet May 15. Sit. Rest. Let the body load.

Visualize: Arriving at obstacles at 165+ bpm and going straight in. The acceleration out of each one. The last km at 175+.
⚠️ One rule above all: If something hurts — not burns, not fatigues, hurts — stop. A niggle on Day 5 can become a DNS on Day 13.
