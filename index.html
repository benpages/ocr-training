Race Simulation Circuit
Full day available — The Money Session
Mixed
Target: 9:00–11:00AM
Session — Most Important Day
3 rounds of:
→ Run 1 full loop (3.2km) at strong effort (~75% HR)
→ On arrival: 10 dips + full monkey bar traverse + 10 burpees
→ Immediately run 800m sprint effort (~85–90% HR)

Why this works
Closest to race conditions you can create. Trains your body to run hard out of an obstacle — exactly where race time is won or lost. Dip/monkey/burpee elevates HR so your 800m sprint starts already hot.
Garmin target
Average HR for running sections: 65–75% max. If you're blowing past that early, dial back loop 1. You need to finish round 3 with something left.

May 16
Friday
🏁 OCR Lev — Race Day
Apartmány Šetkova vila · Anetka's Name Day
Race
Warm-up (20 min before start)
5 min easy jog → dynamic mobility (leg swings, hip openers, arm circles) → 3 × 100m build-up strides → shake out hands and arms. Heart rate elevated, body warm, nothing taxed.
Race Execution
First 1km: Run controlled. Adrenaline will lie to you. Let the first surge pass, settle into your trained tempo pace.
Between obstacles: This is where you attack. Push 75–80% HR. You've trained this exact scenario 10 times.
Into obstacles: Don't brake early. Arrive fast, breathe once, go.
Out of obstacles: Accelerate immediately. Don't let HR settle — get back to race pace within 15 seconds. This is your trained edge.
Final km
Empty the tank. Everything left, all of it. You've trained to have something here — use it all.

Protein

Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, fish, beef.

Carbohydrates

Lighter on recovery days — same protein, less starch.
Fats

Hydration
2.5–3L water daily minimum.
Add electrolytes on hard days — pinch of salt to a bottle.

Big lunch: Pasta or rice + protein + vegetables. Your main fuel load.
Dinner: Lighter, familiar, easy to digest. Chicken + rice. Nothing heavy.
3L water. Electrolytes in the evening. No alcohol.

⚡ May 16 — Race Morning
2–2.5hrs before start: Oats + banana + honey + black coffee. ~500kcal.
30 min before: Small banana or 3–4 dates. Quick carbs only.
Caffeine: 1 strong coffee 45–60 min before start. Proven performance booster.
During (if 50+ min race): 1 gel or handful of dates in a pocket at halfway. Optional, but useful if you feel flat.
Water at every station — small sips, not gulps mid-run.
Sleep — Your #1 Recovery Tool
Target 7.5–8.5 hrs every night. Sleep is when your legs actually get faster, not in the session.

Hard training days (May 4, 7, 9, 11, 13): prioritize 8+ hrs the following night. This is when adaptation happens.

Race week: Two nights before (May 14) matters more than the night before. Bank sleep on the 14th. Pre-race restlessness on the 15th is normal — don't stress it.
Post-Run Protocol — Every Session
Within 5 min of finishing: Walk — don't just stop. Let HR come down over 5 min of movement.

Stretch (10 min minimum): Hip flexors 90 sec each · Hamstrings 60 sec each · Calf/Achilles 60 sec each · Thoracic rotation 10 reps each side.

Cold shower: 2–3 min cold after hard sessions. Reduces inflammation, speeds tissue recovery. Not glamorous. Works.
Legs Management
Foam roll daily: Calves, quads, IT band. 5 min before bed on training days.

Legs-up-the-wall: 10 min after long runs (May 4, 7, 11, 13). Drains fluid, accelerates recovery.

Warning signs: Shin splints, knee tracking issues, calf tightness. If anything spikes in intensity — back off. Arriving at race day at 90% healthy beats arriving at 100% injured.
Nervous System — Don't Ignore This
Sprint work and plyometrics (May 6, 9) are CNS-intensive. You'll feel fine muscularly but flat the next day.

If you feel heavy or unmotivated: That's CNS fatigue, not weakness. It passes in 24 hrs. Eat, sleep, recover as prescribed.

HRV on your Garmin: If it drops 10+ points below your baseline, treat that day as recovery regardless of the plan.
Taper Week — May 14–15
Taper paranoia is real. You will feel slow, stiff, heavy. Your legs are filling with glycogen — they literally feel different. This is good. Don't add extra sessions to "feel better." That is the worst thing you can do.

Stay off your feet on May 15. Sit. Rest. Let the body load up.

Mental prep: Visualize arriving at obstacles breathing hard and completing them without slowing. Visualize the acceleration out of each one. This primes motor patterns.
⚠️ One rule above all: If something hurts (not burns, not fatigues — hurts), stop. You have 13 days. A niggle pushed through on Day 5 can become a DNS on Day 13. Communicate with yourself like a coach, not a soldier.
