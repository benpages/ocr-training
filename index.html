May 11

Threshold Intervals — Beat May 4
Run
Session
Warm up 1km easy — stay under 116 bpm.
6 × 600m at 155–165 bpm. Walk recovery between reps — don't start the next until HR is back under 130 bpm. Walking works; jogging doesn't bring HR down fast enough in 90 seconds.
Cool down 1km easy. Total ~8–9km, ~55 min.
What to look for
Pull up your May 4 Garmin data before you start. Beat every split at the same 155–165 bpm. 10–15 sec/km faster at the same HR = a week of real fitness. That's what you're racing on in 5 days. The data will show it.
Garmin cue
After each rep: note HR at the 60-sec mark of recovery. You want it dropping 20+ bpm in that window. Walking keeps this on track.

May 16
Saturday
🏁 OCR Lev — Race Day
Race
Warm-up (20 min before start)
5 min easy jog → dynamic mobility (leg swings, hip openers, arm circles) → 3 × 100m build-up strides → shake out. HR elevated, body warm, nothing taxed.
Race Execution
First 1km: Adrenaline will push you to 170+ immediately. Let it pass, settle to 155–165 bpm. Running at 175 in the first km kills your last 3km.

Between obstacles: Cruise at 155–165 bpm. It's familiar now — you've trained here all week.

Into obstacles: Don't brake early. Arrive fast, breathe once, execute.

Out of obstacles: Accelerate immediately. Back to 155+ bpm within 15 seconds. This is your trained edge — most people jog it off. You don't.

Last 2km: If HR is under 170, you've been conservative — open it up now.
Final km
Get to 175+ bpm and hold it to the line. You trained to have something here. Use all of it.
Target vs Last Year
Last year: 1:14:08 total · 6:28/km moving · never sprinted · finished with juice.
Today: sub-5:30/km moving pace · sub-63 min total · nothing left at the finish.
Daily Baseline — Every Day
Protein
Target: 1.8–2.2g per kg bodyweight
Every meal has a protein anchor. Non-negotiable.
Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, fish, beef.
Creatine 5g daily — keep your habit straight through race day.
Carbohydrates
Fuel — do not cut these.
Oats, rice, potatoes, fruit, sourdough.
Larger portions on hard days (May 11, 12, 13).
Lighter on recovery days — same protein, less starch.
Fats
Moderate, whole-food based.
Avocado, olive oil, nuts, eggs.
No heavy fat meals within 3hrs of training.
Hydration
2.5–3L water daily minimum.
Add electrolytes on hard days — pinch of salt per bottle.
Urine pale yellow = on track. Dark = behind.
No alcohol. Kills sleep quality measurably.
Training Day Timing — Hard Sessions
Pre-Session (90–60 min before)
Option A: Oats + banana + honey + eggs or whey. ~60g carbs, 20g protein.
Option B (early starts): Banana + dates + black coffee. Train light, eat properly after.
No large meals within 60 min of intense running.
Post-Session (within 45 min)
Protein + fast carbs. Every time.
Greek yogurt + fruit + honey · chicken + rice · protein shake + banana.
This window is where recovery happens. Don't skip it.
Race Week — May 11–16
May 11–13: Carb Build
Increase carbs moderately. Potatoes, rice, pasta, bread.
Topping up glycogen — not gorging. Keep protein high.
No new foods. Nothing your gut doesn't already know.
May 15 — Race Eve
Big lunch: Pasta or rice + protein + vegetables. Main fuel load.
Dinner: Light, familiar, easy to digest. Chicken + rice.
3L water. Electrolytes in the evening. No alcohol.
⚡ May 16 — Race Morning
2–2.5hrs before start: Oats + banana + honey + black coffee. ~500kcal.
30 min before: Small banana or 3–4 dates. Quick carbs only.
Caffeine: 1 strong coffee 45–60 min before start.
During: 1 gel or handful of dates at halfway if you feel flat.
Water stations: Small sips, not gulps mid-run.
Sleep — Your #1 Recovery Tool
7.5–8.5 hrs every night. Sleep is when your legs get faster, not in the session.

After hard days (May 11, 12, 13): 8+ hrs the following night. Non-negotiable. Adaptation happens here.

Race week: May 14 night matters more than May 15 night. Bank sleep on the 14th. Pre-race restlessness on the 15th is normal.
Post-Run Protocol — Every Session
Within 5 min of finishing: Walk — don't stop dead. HR down over 5 min of movement.

Stretch minimum 10 min: Hip flexors 90 sec each · Hamstrings 60 sec each · Calf/Achilles 60 sec each · Thoracic rotation 10 reps each side.

Cold shower: 2–3 min cold after hard sessions. Reduces inflammation, speeds tissue recovery.
Legs Management
Foam roll daily: Calves, quads, IT band. 5 min before bed on training days.

Legs-up-the-wall: 10 min after long runs (May 11, 13). Drains fluid, accelerates recovery.

Warning signs: Shin splints, knee tracking issues, sharp calf tightness — back off immediately. 90% healthy beats 100% injured.
Nervous System
Threshold intervals and circuit work (May 11, 12) are CNS-intensive. You'll feel muscularly fine but flat the next day — normal.

HRV on Garmin: If it drops 10+ points below your baseline, treat that day as recovery regardless of the plan.
Taper Days — May 14–15
Taper paranoia is real. Heavy, slow, stiff legs = glycogen loading. This is good. Do not add sessions to feel better — worst thing you can do.

Stay off your feet May 15. Sit. Rest. Let the body load.

Visualize: Arriving at obstacles at 165+ bpm and going straight in. The acceleration out of each one. The last km at 175+.
⚠️ One rule above all: If something hurts — not burns, not fatigues, hurts — stop. A niggle today can become a DNS on Saturday.
